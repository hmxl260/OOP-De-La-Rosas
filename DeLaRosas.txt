[African violet:]
    @ Common Name: African Violet (syn. Saintpaulia)
    @ Scientific Name: Streptocarpus ionanthus (formerly Saintpaulia ionantha)
    @ Cultivar: –
    @ ----
    @ Family: Gesneriaceae
    @ Genus: Streptocarpus
    @ Species: ionanthus (one of several in the section Saintpaulia)
    @ ----
    @ Floriography: Symbolizes devotion, commitment, and faithfulness; often given as a token of loyalty and maternal love. African violets’ soft, humble blooms are associated with modesty and spiritual wisdom (per general floral symbolism of violets)
    @ Etymology: Genus Saintpaulia named for Baron Walter von Saint-Paul-Illaire who introduced the plant to Europe; species epithet ionantha from Greek ion (“violet”) + anthos (“flower”). (The current genus Streptocarpus means “twisted fruit” from Greek roots.)
    @ ----
    @ Flower Color/s: Wide range of purple, violet, pink, blue, white (often bicolored)
    @ Petal Count/s: 5 petals (5 lobes per flower) on a typical African violet bloom
    @ Petal Shape/s: Rounded to slightly obovate with scalloped edges; blooms are saucer-shaped.
    @ Fragrance: Generally none (African violets are usually unscented).
    @ Height: Compact form to ~15–20 cm tall (varies by variety)
    @ Stem Type: Fleshy herbaceous stems, often forming a rosette of leaves; stems are succulent and fibrous.
    @ Root Type: Fibrous, shallow (hence grow well in shallow pots).
    @ Bloom Shape: Rounded clusters of flat-faced, symmetric flowers; each flower has a slight cuplike shape.
    @ Texture: Velvety (leaves and petals have fine hairs)
    @ ----
    @ Bloom Calendar: Blooms year-round in good indoor conditions; peak flowering in spring-summer; often continuous bloom under steady care
    @ Seasonal Conditions: Prefers bright indirect light and warm indoor temperatures (18–24°C); requires steady moisture and humidity
    @ Natural Habitat: Moist cloud forests of Tanzania (East Africa), on shaded forest floor near streams

[Cymbidium (Orchid):]
    @ Common Name: Cymbidium Orchid
    @ Scientific Name: Cymbidium spp. (e.g. C. goeringii)
    @ Cultivar: –
    @ ----
    @ Family: Orchidaceae (orchid family)【source】
    @ Genus: Cymbidium
    @ Species: multiple (over 50 species, plus many hybrids)
    @ ----
    @ Floriography: Orchids symbolize refinement, exotic beauty, and strength; specifically, cymbidiums often represent virtue and moral excellence.
    @ Etymology: From Greek kymbe (“boat”) referring to the boat-shaped lip of the flower.
    @ ----
    @ Flower Color/s: Green, yellow, pink, red, brown, variegated (often spotted or striped).
    @ Petal Count/s: 3 petals and 3 sepals; one petal modified into a lip (labellum).
    @ Petal Shape/s: Depending on species, can be star-shaped or with broad lip; sepals and petals often similar.
    @ Fragrance: Some hybrids are fragrant; wild species C. goeringii notably sweet-smelling.
    @ Height: 30–60 cm (pseudobulb-bearing leaves tall).
    @ Stem Type: Pseudobulbs (swollen stem bases) bearing leaves; flower stalk arises from pseudobulbs.
    @ Root Type: Aerial, fibrous roots (like most orchids).
    @ Bloom Shape: Long sprays of multiple orchid blooms on arching inflorescences.
    @ Texture: Waxy smooth petals.
    @ ----
    @ Bloom Calendar: Usually winter or spring (some flower in fall); blooms last weeks.
    @ Seasonal Conditions: Cool growing conditions for some species; bright but indirect light; grown as greenhouse plant in temperate climates.
    @ Natural Habitat: Native to Asia, Australia, and Pacific islands (grasslands and forests).

[Dahlia:]

[Daisy:]
    @ Common Name: Daisy (common daisy)
    @ Scientific Name: Bellis perennis (English daisy) or generically any similar flower (e.g., Leucanthemum vulgare, Gerbera jamesonii).
    @ Cultivar: –
    @ ----
    @ Family: Asteraceae (daisy family)【source】
    @ Genus: Bellis (for B. perennis)
    @ Species: perennis (for English daisy)
    @ ----
    @ Floriography: Symbolizes innocence, purity, and loyal love; daisies are often given to express innocence or unconditional love.

    @ ----
    @ Bloom Calendar: Early spring through summer (English daisy); others summer peak.
    @ Seasonal Conditions: Sun to part shade; cool-season hardy.
    @ Natural Habitat: Europe (English daisy naturalized widely), widely cultivated species globally.

[Delphinium:]
    @ Common Name: Delphinium, Larkspur
    @ Scientific Name: Delphinium elatum (tall larkspur) and others
    @ Cultivar: –
    @ ----
    @ Family: Ranunculaceae (buttercup family)【source】
    @ Genus: Delphinium
    @ Species: D. elatum (common tall hybrids)
    @ ----

    @ Bloom Calendar: Early summer (June-July).

[Dianthus:]
    @ Common Name: Carnation (Dianthus); includes Sweet William, Pinks, etc.
    @ Scientific Name: Dianthus caryophyllus (carnation), D. barbatus (Sweet William), D. plumarius (garden pinks)
    @ Cultivar: –
    @ ----
    @ Family: Caryophyllaceae (pink family)【source】
    @ Genus: Dianthus
    @ Species: various (garden sweet william, carnations, pinks)
    @ ----
    @ Floriography: Carnations symbolize love (especially pink for motherly love), fascination, and distinction; Sweet William represents gallantry. Pinks (D. plumarius) symbolize purity and good luck. Overall the genus stands for boldness and passion.
    @ Etymology: Dianthus from Greek dios anthos meaning “flower of Zeus” (among the gods). caryophyllus means “nut-kernel leaf” (clove-scented leaves).
    @ ----
    @ Flower Color/s: Red, pink, white, yellow, green, bicolor; heavily cultured array.
    @ Petal Count/s: 5 (often fringed or notched)【source】.
    @ Petal Shape/s: Round petals with fringed or smooth margins. Carnations have many overlapping petals in double blooms; Sweet William flat clusters.
    @ Fragrance: Sweet, spicy clove-like scent (especially carnations).
    @ Height: 30–90 cm (carnations ~40-60 cm, Sweet William ~20-60 cm).
    @ Stem Type: Erect, leafy stems (often woody base in carnations).
    @ Root Type: Fibrous (perennials); biennial (Sweet William).
    @ Bloom Shape: Frilly, full carnations; flat-topped umbels in Sweet William; single in border pinks.
    @ Texture: Soft, ruffled petals; fragrance note is spicy.
    @ ----
    @ Bloom Calendar: Carnations and Sweet Williams bloom late spring to summer; border pinks late spring.
    @ Seasonal Conditions: Full sun; well-drained alkaline soil; carnations often grown as annual/perennial.
    @ Natural Habitat: Europe and Asia (rocky areas, grassy slopes).
